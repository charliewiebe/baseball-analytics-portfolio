About Me

Hi — I’m Charlie Wiebe. I recently graduated from Willamette University, where I played baseball and earned both my Bachelor’s and Master’s degrees in Data Science. While my recent professional work has been public health-centered, including a project at Oregon Health & Science University, where I created an agentic AI pipeline to aid with prostate cancer diagnosis, I’ve recently been drawn back to the field that originally sparked my interest in data science: baseball analytics.

This portfolio displays a few baseball-focused projects I’ve worked on recently, combining: - Statistical modeling (run value, pitch models, decision models)
- Machine learning (XGBoost, clustering, classification)
- R-based data engineering & visualization
- Baseball domain knowledge grounded in process over outcomes

My goal is to create tools that help coaches, analysts, and players make better decisions
— from swing decisions and pitch shapes to defensive positioning and player development.
